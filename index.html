üîç Understand Smoking Behavior

Understanding your smoking behavior is the foundation of successful cessation.

  • Smoking Patterns: When, where, and why you smoke, including routines and daily situations.
  • Triggers: Emotional, environmental, and physical cues that create the urge to smoke.
  • Dependencies: How physical addiction, psychological needs, and social factors work together.
  • Rationalizations: Thoughts or beliefs that make smoking seem acceptable or justified.
  • Personality and Traits: How individual differences influence smoking habits.
  • Cognitive Processes: How attention, memory, and decision-making affect smoking choices.
  • Social and Environmental Factors: How peers, family, culture, and surroundings shape smoking behavior.
  • Relapse Dynamics: Why relapses occur and common situations involved.

üìã Assessment Scales

Our assessment scales measure different dimensions:

  • Nicotine Dependence Check: Measures the intensity of physical dependence on nicotine.
  • Withdrawal Symptom Rating: Assesses the severity of nicotine withdrawal effects.
  • Smoking Motivation Assessment: Evaluates key reasons and triggers behind smoking behavior.
  • Motivation to Quit Survey: Determines readiness and commitment to quit.
  • Personality Profile Test: Explores personality traits that influence smoking.
  • Genetic Predisposition Assessment: Estimates biological tendencies related to nicotine use.
